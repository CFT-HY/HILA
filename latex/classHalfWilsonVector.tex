class Half\+Wilson\+Vector$<$ N, T $>$}
Multiplication with gamma matrices. 

half\+\_\+\+Wilson\+\_\+vector is a Wilson vector projected by 1 +-\/ gamma\+\_\+j and contains half the degrees of freedom

(1 +-\/ gamma\+\_\+j) is a projection operator. We will apply the projection to a Wilson\+\_\+vector and store the result in a half\+\_\+\+Wilson\+\_\+vector. This will store all necessary information in half the amount of data and reduce the effort of multiplying with gauge matrices by half.

The constructor half\+\_\+\+Wilson\+\_\+vector(\+Wilson\+\_\+vector$<$n, radix$>$ w, Direction d) will take the projection automatically. A positive Direction d corresponds to 1+gamma\+\_\+j and a negative Direction d corresponds to 1-\/gamma\+\_\+j.

A half\+\_\+\+Wilson\+\_\+vector can be expanded to a full Wilson\+\_\+vector using the method expand(\+Direction d). Notice that this requires knowing the Direction that was used to project it. The Direction is not stored.

Note that the eigenvectors below are normalized to sqrt(2), or \texorpdfstring{$\vert$}{|}v\texorpdfstring{$\ast$}{*}v\texorpdfstring{$\vert$}{|} = 2. This is why we don\textquotesingle{}t explicitly multiply by 2 when expanding to full Wilson\+\_\+vector.
